Why LUMUS for Your Child

Most psychiatric care for children starts with a symptom and ends with a prescription.

We start somewhere different.

Before we consider any intervention, we consider your child as a complete human being — shaped by their biology, their relationships, their family, their school, their culture, and the world they are navigating right now. That is not how most child psychiatry works. It is how it should work.

Dr. Garcia is double board-certified in both adult psychiatry and child and adolescent psychiatry. That combination matters. It means she understands child development not as a subspecialty she rotated through, but as a primary discipline — and it means she can provide continuity into adulthood for patients who want to stay.

Our Philosophy

We treat the whole child.
Not just the diagnosis.

Dr. Garcia's approach is grounded in the biopsychosocialcultural model. That means every dimension of your child's life is considered before any clinical decision is made: their biology, their psychology, their family system, their school environment, their cultural context, and their individual sense of who they are becoming.

This is not a checklist. It is a genuine commitment to understanding your child before treating them.

What that looks like in practice: longer appointments, real conversation with both child and parent, an honest clinical formulation, and a treatment plan that is built around your child's actual life — not a standardized protocol.

The goal is not a child who is easier to manage. The goal is a child who understands themselves, feels safe in their relationships, and has the tools to navigate the hard parts of growing up.

How We Work

Evidence-based. Individually tailored. Never one-size-fits-all.

Dr. Garcia draws from a full toolkit of evidence-based therapeutic modalities — selecting and sequencing interventions based on your child's specific needs, developmental stage, and family context. No single approach works for every child. This one is built around yours.

Evidence-Based Toolkit
Group A

Child-Specific Interventions

  • Parent Management Training (PMT) — teaching parents evidence-based strategies to respond to difficult behavior
  • Parent-Child Interaction Therapy (PCIT) — structured work on the parent-child relationship for younger children
  • Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT) — processing trauma in a structured, developmentally appropriate way
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — identifying and changing unhelpful thought and behavior patterns
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) — emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal skills
Group B

Additional Modalities

  • Internal Family Systems (IFS) — understanding the different parts of the self in conflict
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) — building psychological flexibility and values-based action
  • Mindfulness-based interventions — attention, self-regulation, and present-moment awareness
  • Supportive therapy — relationship-based support through difficult periods
  • Family therapy — working with the family system, not just the identified patient
  • School consultation — engaging directly with school staff and support teams when clinically appropriate
  • Nutritional and lifestyle guidance — sleep, nutrition, and movement as foundational treatment elements
We also spend time helping your child discover what matters to them — purpose, direction, and a sense of identity that is their own. For many adolescents, that work is not incidental to healing. It is the healing.

A Note for Parents

You are not just bringing your child.
You are part of the work.

Child psychiatry done well is never just about the child in the room. It is about the family system, the relational environment, and the adults who are closest to that child every day.

That means we will ask you questions — not to evaluate your parenting, but because your perspective is clinical data. It means we will give you tools and frameworks, not just diagnoses. And it means that when the work requires it, we will work directly with you alongside your child.

Parent Consultation Built In

Every treatment plan includes regular parent contact. You will always know what we are working on and why.

School Coordination Available

When clinically appropriate, Dr. Garcia can communicate directly with school counselors, teachers, and IEP teams to ensure your child's environment supports their treatment.

Telehealth Scheduling That Works for Families

School schedules, work schedules, and the reality of getting a teenager to an appointment are all factors. Telehealth makes consistency possible.
